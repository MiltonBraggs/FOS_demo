What is FOSS

FOSS stands for Free and Open Source Software. It refers to computer software that is made freely available to the public with the source code included.

Benefits of FOSS

One of the biggest advantages of FOSS is that it is often much cheaper than proprietary software. Since FOSS is made freely available to the public, there are no licensing fees to pay.

History of FOSS

FOSS include the GNU project, which was started in 1983 by Richard Stallman, and the Linux operating system, which was first released in 1991 by Linus Torvalds.

FOSS alternatives to common applications

There are many FOSS alternatives to common applications, eg: Linux, LibreOffice, GIMP etc.
