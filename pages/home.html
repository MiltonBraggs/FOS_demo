What is FOSS

FOSS stands for Free and Open Source Software. It refers to computer software that is made freely available to the public with the source code included.

Benefits of FOSS

One of the biggest advantages of FOSS is that it is often much cheaper than proprietary software.


History of FOSS

FOSS include the GNU project, which was started by Richard Stallman, and the Linux os, which was released Linus Torvalds.

FOSS alternatives to common applications

There are many FOSS alternatives to common applications, eg: Linux, LibreOffice, GIMP etc.

Ties with GNU/Linux

FOSS and Linux have a close relationship, as Linux is one of the most well-known and widely used examples of FOSS.
